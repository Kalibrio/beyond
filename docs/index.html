// Why now

What if humanity is not alone — and the proof was here all along?

The world has changed. Governments are unsealing archives. Military witnesses are going on the record. Ancient stories are being read again, in a new light. For the first time, millions of people are asking the same question out loud — and a provocative theory is gathering force: that somewhere on Earth lie a handful of places where contact between humanity and a non-human intelligence has happened for centuries.

One of those places is said to be hidden deep in the Pyrenees. For generations, shepherds, monks, smugglers, pilgrims and villagers have told of strange lights, impossible visitors, underground passages and forgotten monasteries. Most dismiss it as folklore. A small group decides to find out for themselves.

What begins as an investigation becomes a treasure hunt, a spiritual quest, and a test of friendship. As coincidences multiply and the line between myth and reality blurs, the team realises that finding the base may matter far less than understanding why it exists — or who wants it found.

Tone & style

Shot like an expedition. Felt like a séance.

Grounded documentary realism that slips, episode by episode, into something stranger. The camera believes in nothing and films everything — until the footage stops cooperating. Prestige docu-fiction with the pulse of an adventure and the unease of a ghost story, anchored by real people, real terrain, and a movement that already owns the cultural moment.

The expedition

An unlikely team.

Eight days. One mountain range. A handful of seekers with nothing in common but the need to know.

01

Entrepreneur, author, and relentless explorer of unconventional ideas. He doesn't believe — he needs to know. The expedition is his.

02

A Canadian filmmaker documenting the journey, fighting to stay objective as the lens keeps catching things he can't explain.

03
Cedric
The Pattern

A mathematical prodigy convinced the geometry hidden in ancient sites is a signature, not a coincidence — and that it's still broadcasting.

04
Karl
The Naturalist

From Martinique, he reads bees, ecosystems and indigenous wisdom as one language — a way of interpreting reality the others have forgotten.

05
Sophie
The Frontier

A former gaming executive turned shaman, certain that consciousness itself — not technology — is the true frontier, and the real gateway.

06
The Others
The Drift

A Portuguese mystic, his German companion Mike, and the seekers who attach themselves to the trail — each carrying a piece nobody asked for.

Ensemble note

Five points of view that can't all be right. Skeptic, believer, scientist, naturalist, mystic — the season's engine is the friction between ways of seeing, and what happens to a friendship when the mountain answers back.

// The series engine

Beyond Season One

The Pyrenees is one node. Each season moves to a new suspected contact zone — and the closer the team looks, the more the locations stop feeling like coincidences.

SEASON 2
Antarctica

A classified scientific anomaly beneath the ice leads the team to a second contact zone, hidden under a forgotten research installation.

SEASON 3
The Abyss

Ancient shipwrecks, lost civilisations and unexplained underwater structures point toward a submerged gateway in the Atlantic.

SEASON 4
The Desert

Satellite imagery and indigenous oral histories converge on a remote region where reality itself appears unstable.

SEASON 5
The Network

The evidence resolves: all four sites are connected through a planetary system older than recorded human history.

The truth was never hidden.
We simply didn't know how to see it.
